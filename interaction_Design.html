The Project

For my Interaction Design course, I decided to design a prototype for an app to help students commute to college campuses easier.

Students are stressed, tired, and never have enough time to get everything done. Wasting time on something as trivial as finding parking should not add to the already bursting workload of most students. Many college campuses offer parking lots for students if they buy parking passes, but in my experience these lots fill up quickly and students are forced to drive around from lot to lot, looking for an open space so they can use the parking pass that they paid hundreds of dollars for.
So I created a prototype for an app that displays the availability of parking at campus parking lots. This kind of infrastructure exists all over the world at private parking lots, so there's really no excuse for colleges that charge obscene intuition fees to not have it. The app also tries to enable students to set up consistent carpools with one another, allowing students to set a weekly schedule and look for other students going to campus at a similar time.
